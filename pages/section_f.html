Section F

Sustainability & Societal Impact

An overview of how NLP contributes to inclusion, access, and environmentally responsible computing.

Return to overview

NLP is more than a technical field. It supports multilingual access, assistive tools, and better public services by turning language into a medium that machines can help interpret and distribute. In practical terms, that means wider access to education, health information, and government services for users who are otherwise left out by language barriers.

It also matters for sustainability because large language systems can be expensive to train and serve. Efficient models, distillation, retrieval-based approaches, and careful deployment help reduce waste while keeping the benefits of language technology available to more people.
